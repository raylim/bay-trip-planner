Initiating Kayak Trips

Based on the comprehensive guide from BASK.org, here's what you need to know about starting a bay kayaking trip.

1. Planning & Preparation

  • Check weather and tidal conditions
  • Review your route and identify bailout points
  • Notify someone of your trip plan
  • Ensure all gear is in good condition

2. Safety Considerations

  • Always wear a properly fitted PFD (Personal Flotation Device)
  • Dress for water temperature, not air temperature
  • Carry essential safety equipment
  • Know your skills and limitations

3. Essential Equipment

  • Kayak and paddle suitable for bay conditions
  • PFD and appropriate clothing
  • Navigation tools (map, compass, GPS)
  • Communication device (VHF radio or cell phone in waterproof case)
  • Safety gear (whistle, light, first aid kit)

4. Group Paddling

  • Never paddle alone in the bay
  • Establish communication signals
  • Stay together as a group
  • Designate a trip leader

Suggested Paddle Trips

Explore the San Francisco Bay with these recommended paddle routes from BASK's "10 Paddles Around the Bay" guide. Each itinerary offers unique scenery and challenges for different skill levels.

China Camp to the Sisters

Distance: 3 miles round trip

Skill Level: Beginner to Intermediate

A scenic short paddle in San Pablo Bay. Easy launch from China Camp State Park. Watch for mud at low tide and fishing lines at McNears Beach. Channel currents between The Sister Islands create interesting eddies.

Tip: Post-paddle dining available in San Rafael or at Marin Brewing Company

Point Richmond to Brooks Island

Distance: 5.5 miles round trip

Skill Level: Intermediate

Brooks Island is a bird reserve (no landings allowed). Challenging conditions when winds are from the southwest - good bracing skills required. Cross shipping channels at right angles and watch for ship traffic.

Tip: Launch from Point Richmond with parking and restrooms. Great dining options in town afterward

Crissy Field to The Ramp

Distance: 7 miles one way

Skill Level: Intermediate

Urban paddle along San Francisco's waterfront, passing historic piers and under the Bay Bridge. Ends at The Ramp restaurant (requires shuttle for cars). Watch for sailboat races and fast ferries. Limited parking at The Ramp.

Tip: Scenic route with plenty of city sights and dining at the finish

The Farallon Islands

Distance: 30 miles round trip

Skill Level: Expert/Expedition

Epic open ocean paddle for very experienced kayakers only. No landings allowed on the islands. Hazards include big ships, fog, waves, high winds, and sharks. Launch from points west of San Francisco. Requires expedition-level preparation.

Warning: Only for highly experienced ocean kayakers with proper training and equipment

Important: Always read BASK's kayaking safety tips before embarking on any paddle. Conditions can change rapidly in the Bay, so prepare for worst-case scenarios and carry appropriate safety gear.

Trip Planner - Tides & Currents

Understanding tides and currents is essential for safe bay kayaking. Use the BASK trip planner to check conditions for your route.

Why Tides and Currents Matter

The San Francisco Bay experiences significant tidal currents that can reach speeds of 3-6 knots or more, especially near the Golden Gate. Planning your trip around slack tides or favorable currents can make the difference between an enjoyable paddle and a dangerous situation.

Key Considerations:

  • Slack tide: The period between tidal changes when currents are minimal - ideal for crossing channels
  • Flood tide: Incoming tide - water flows into the bay from the ocean
  • Ebb tide: Outgoing tide - water flows out of the bay toward the ocean
  • Current speed: Stronger during spring tides, weaker during neap tides

Planning Your Route:

  1. Identify your launch and destination points
  2. Check tide predictions for your area
  3. Plan to paddle with the current when possible
  4. Schedule crossings during slack tide
  5. Build in extra time for unexpected conditions
